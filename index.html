Questions
Frequently Asked Questions
Where does the data come from?
Every dimension uses named institutional sources: WHO and Lancet GBD for health, Yale EPI and WMO for environment, UN World Happiness Report for happiness, Global Peace Index for safety, JCI and WHO for healthcare, Numbeo for cost, and Speedtest/EF EPI/InterNations/ITU for connectivity. No proprietary or made-up scores.
Why is Health weighted at 30%?
Because this is a longevity index, not a cost-of-living guide. Life expectancy and disease burden (from WHO/Lancet) are the most important factors in determining where you'll live longest. Cities with world-class health infrastructure naturally score higher — exactly as they should.
Is this a subscription?
No. It's a one-time payment of $9.99 (founding price). You get lifetime access to all current and future data, including new cities as we expand toward 500+.
Why are there so many Thai cities in the ranking?
Thailand genuinely scores high on visa accessibility (multiple long-stay visa programs), peace & safety (GPI rank #33), healthcare quality (JCI-accredited hospitals), cost of living, and happiness. However, Health (D1 at 30%) keeps the ranking balanced — Thai cities score 63-68 on health vs. 80-86 for developed nations. The result is a fair, data-driven outcome.
How often is the data updated?
We update scores whenever source institutions release new data (typically annually for WHO, Lancet, Yale EPI, and UN WHR). New cities are added quarterly as we expand toward our goal of 500+ cities.
What's the difference between V10 and earlier versions?
V10 is a complete rebuild. We reduced 17 factors to 8 globally-credible dimensions, removed all proprietary metrics, required named institutional sources for every score, added 8 new cities, and rebalanced weights for global fairness. It's the most rigorous version we've ever released.
Can I get a refund?
Yes. 30-day money-back guarantee, no questions asked. If the data doesn't help you make a better decision about where to live, we'll refund you in full.
